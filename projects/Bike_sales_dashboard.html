Project Overview

This Excel dashboard analyzes bike sales data to uncover customer demographics and purchasing patterns. Using pivot tables, slicers, and data visualization, it reveals key insights about what drives bike purchases across different customer segments.

The dashboard enables users to:

  • Filter data dynamically by marital status, region, and education
  • Compare income levels across customer groups
  • Analyze age-based purchasing trends
  • Evaluate how commute distance impacts sales

Key Features & Analysis

  • Demographic Trends

    Middle-aged customers emerge as the most frequent buyers, with males showing higher purchase rates than females.

  • Geographic Insights

    North America dominates sales, and customers living closer to bike shops demonstrate higher purchase frequency.

  • Income Patterns

    Income brackets correlate with purchasing behavior, with mid-to-high income earners most likely to buy.

  • Interactive Exploration

    Slicers allow dynamic filtering by marital status, region, education, and occupation for tailored analysis.

  • Commute Impact

    Shorter commute distances strongly correlate with increased purchase likelihood.

Technical Implementation

The analysis was built with:

  • Excel data cleaning and preparation techniques
  • Pivot tables for multi-dimensional analysis
  • Slicers for interactive dashboard filtering
  • Conditional formatting to highlight key metrics
  • Custom charts for comparative visualization
